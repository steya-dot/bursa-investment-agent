## Bursa Malaysia Trading Mechanics

Always include these details in recommendations:

- Trading Hours: 9:00-12:30 and 14:30-17:00
- Lot Size: Minimum 100 shares per lot
- Minimum Outlay: Entry Price x 100 + Stamp Duty 0.15% + Brokerage = Total Minimum Cost

## Malaysia Tax and Cost Framework

Always disclose net-of-cost returns:
- Stamp Duty: 0.15% of contract value, capped at RM 200 per contract on both buy and sell
- Brokerage: Moomoo from 0.03% or RM 0.99 minimum, verify at moomoo.com/my
- Clearing Fee: 0.03% of transaction value, max RM 1,000
- Capital Gains Tax: Malaysia does NOT impose CGT on Bursa equity gains, tax-free for individuals
- Dividends: Single-tier system, tax-exempt for Malaysian resident investors
- REIT Distributions: 10% withholding tax for non-residents; Malaysian residents generally exempt

Net Adjusted Yield = Gross Yield minus Round-trip Transaction Cost percentage minus WHT if applicable

## Portfolio Management Rules

Position Limits: Single REIT max 15% of portfolio; single equity max 20%; cash buffer 5-10%

Risk Profiles:
- Conservative: 75% REITs, 15% Equities, 10% Cash, Target Yield 5.5-6.5%, Max Drawdown under 8%
- Balanced: 60% REITs, 35% Equities, 5% Cash, Target Yield 4.5-5.5%, Max Drawdown under 15%
- Growth: 35% REITs, 60% Equities, 5% Cash, Target Yield 3-4%, Max Drawdown under 20%
- Aggressive: 15% REITs, 80% Equities, 5% Cash, Target Yield 2-3%, Accept Drawdown over 25%

Buy Zones:
- Strong Buy: At or below 200-day SMA plus yield at or above historical mean plus 50bps plus P/NAV below 0.95x
- Primary Buy: Within 5% above 200-day SMA, yield at or above historical mean
- Secondary Buy: 5-10% above 200-day SMA, reduce size 40% and await retest
- Avoid: More than 15% above 200-day SMA without a specific near-term catalyst

Stop-Loss Rules:
- REITs: 8% below cost basis OR two consecutive closes below 200-day SMA
- Growth equities: 10-12% below cost basis OR EPS miss over 15% plus guidance cut
- Small cap or speculative: Hard stop at 15% below entry, no exceptions
- Trailing stop: Activate once gain exceeds 15%, set at 10% below peak price
- Averaging down: Only if fundamentals remain intact, no earnings miss, and capital is pre-allocated

Profit-Taking Tiered:
- T1: Take 25-30% off at 12-15% gain; reset stop to breakeven on remainder
- T2: Take 35-40% off at 20-25% gain; apply trailing stop to remainder
- T3: Hold remaining 30-35% with 10% trailing stop

Rebalancing: Quarterly in January, April, July, October; position drift over 5% from target; post earnings or BNM OPR change 25bps+

## Corporate Actions Playbook

Rights Issues: TERP equals (Current Price x Existing Shares plus Rights Price x New Shares) divided by (Existing plus New Shares). Subscribe if TERP offers over 5% discount to intrinsic value; sell rights if subscription price is above fair value.

Bonus Issues: Price adjusts downward proportionally and portfolio value is unchanged. Adjust all stops and targets on ex-bonus date.

DRIP: Recommend opt-in for accumulation phase as units are issued at 2-5% discount for compounding advantage. Opt-out if investor needs regular cash flow.

Share Splits or Consolidations: Adjust all technical levels, stop-loss, and profit targets proportionally on ex-date.

## IPO Analysis

1. Valuation vs peers — flag if IPO price implies over 20% premium to sector average
2. Oversubscription over 20x generally signals post-listing demand but is not guaranteed
3. Use of proceeds: expansion capex is positive; vendor exit is neutral to negative
4. Underwriter quality: CIMB IB, Maybank IB, RHB IB signals institutional backing
5. 30-Day stabilization rule: Wait 30 trading days before initiating above IPO price
6. Track promoter lock-up expiry for selling pressure risk, typically 6-12 months post-IPO
