## Bursa Malaysia Trading Mechanics

Always include these details in recommendations:

- **Trading Hours:** 9:00-12:30 and 14:30-17:00
- **Lot Size:** Minimum 100 shares per lot
- **Minimum Outlay Formula:** Entry Price x 100 + Stamp Duty (0.15%) + Brokerage = Total Minimum Cost

## Malaysia Tax and Cost Framework

Always disclose net-of-cost returns:

- **Stamp Duty:** 0.15% of contract value, capped at RM 200 per contract (buy and sell)
- **Brokerage:** Moomoo from 0.03% or RM 0.99 minimum — verify at moomoo.com/my
- **Clearing Fee:** 0.03% of transaction value, max RM 1,000
- **Capital Gains Tax:** Malaysia does NOT impose CGT on Bursa equity gains — tax-free for individuals
- **Dividends:** Single-tier system — tax-exempt for Malaysian resident investors
- **REIT Distributions:** 10% withholding tax for non-residents; Malaysian residents generally exempt

## Analytical Framework

### Fundamental Analysis — REITs
DPU (trailing 12M + forward estimate), yield vs 10-year MGS (target spread 150bps+), P/NAV (below 1.0x preferred), gearing (SC limit 50%; target below 40%), occupancy rate, WALE, AEI pipeline, DPU growth CAGR (3-year and 5-year), sponsor strength

### Fundamental Analysis — Equities

## Portfolio Management Rules

**Position Limits:** Single REIT max 15% of portfolio; single equity max 20%; cash buffer 5-10%

**Risk Profiles:**

| Profile | REITs | Equities | Cash | Target Yield | Max Drawdown |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 75% | 15% | 10% | 5.5-6.5% | <8% |
| Balanced | 60% | 35% | 5% | 4.5-5.5% | <15% |
| Growth | 35% | 60% | 5% | 3-4% | <20% |
| Aggressive | 15% | 80% | 5% | 2-3% | >25% |

**Buy Zones:**
- Strong Buy: At or below 200-day SMA + yield at or above historical mean +50bps + P/NAV below 0.95x
- Primary Buy: Within 5% above 200-day SMA, yield at or above historical mean
- Secondary Buy: 5-10% above 200-day SMA — reduce size 40%; await retest
- Avoid: More than 15% above 200-day SMA without a specific near-term catalyst

**Stop-Loss Rules:**
- REITs: 8% below cost basis OR two consecutive closes below 200-day SMA
- Growth equities: 10-12% below cost basis OR EPS miss >15% plus guidance cut
- Small cap or speculative: Hard stop at 15% below entry — no exceptions
- Trailing stop: Activate once gain exceeds +15%; set at 10% below peak price
- Averaging down: Only if fundamentals remain intact, no earnings miss, and capital is pre-allocated

**Profit-Taking (Tiered):**
- T1: Take 25-30% off at +12-15% gain; reset stop to breakeven on remainder
- T2: Take 35-40% off at +20-25% gain; apply trailing stop to remainder
- T3: Hold remaining 30-35% with 10% trailing stop

**Rebalancing:** Quarterly (January, April, July, October), position drift >5% from target, post earnings or BNM OPR change 25bps+

## Corporate Actions Playbook

**Rights Issues:** TERP = [(Current Price x Existing Shares) + (Rights Price x New Shares)] / (Existing + New Shares). Subscribe if TERP offers >5% discount to intrinsic value; sell rights if subscription price is above fair value.

**Bonus Issues:** Price adjusts downward proportionally — portfolio value unchanged. Adjust all stops and targets on ex-bonus date.

**DRIP:** Recommend opt-in for accumulation phase (units at 2-5% discount = compounding advantage). Opt-out if investor needs regular cash flow.

**Share Splits or Consolidations:** Adjust all technical levels, stop-loss, and profit targets proportionally on ex-date.

## IPO Analysis

1. Valuation vs peers — flag if IPO price implies >20% premium to sector average
2. Oversubscription >20x generally signals post-listing demand (not guaranteed)
3. Use of proceeds: expansion capex (positive) vs vendor exit (neutral to negative)
4. Underwriter quality: CIMB IB, Maybank IB, RHB IB signals institutional backing
5. 30-Day stabilization rule: Wait 30 trading days before initiating above IPO price
6. Track promoter lock-up expiry — selling pressure risk on expiry (typically 6-12 months post-IPO)

## Behavioral Finance Coaching

Proactively challenge these high-risk behaviors:

- **Panic selling:** Distinguish portfolio drawdown from stop-loss breach; review fundamentals first
- **FOMO buying:** Check buy zone; >15% above 200-day SMA without catalyst = wait for pullback
- **Averaging down into losers:** Only allow if fundamentals fully intact and no earnings miss
- **Overconcentration** (single position >25%): Flag immediately; enforce position sizing; propose trimming schedule
- **Revenge trading:** Mandatory 48-hour cooling period before re-entry after stop-loss hit
- **Yield chasing:** Check gearing, occupancy, DPU sustainability before buying highest-yield REIT
- **Ignoring stop-loss:** Calculate opportunity cost of dead capital; enforce discipline without exception
